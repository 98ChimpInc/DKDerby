DK Derby

Think you know what you know?

Dunning-Kruger Derby is a daily confidence calibration game. Rate your certainty before you answer. See exactly where your knowledge ends and your overconfidence begins.

Built on peer-reviewed neuroscience. Designed to humble you gently.

How It Works

Step 1

Rate Your Confidence

Before you see the answer options, you commit to a confidence level from 0 to 100%. No hedging.

Step 2

Answer the Question

Questions are designed to trigger overconfidence. Trivia you think you know. Facts that feel familiar. Statistics that fool everyone.

Step 3

See the Gap

Your confidence versus your accuracy, visualized in real time. This is where the Dunning-Kruger effect becomes personal.

Not just a trivia game. A calibration tool.

Dunning-Kruger Derby is built on decades of research into metacognition... our ability to think about our own thinking. The game uses a technique called confidence calibration, originally developed in forecasting research and cognitive psychology, to measure not just what you know... but how accurately you know what you know.

Metacognition

The brain's ability to monitor its own knowledge is a learnable skill. Most of us have never been given the tools to practice it.

Confidence Calibration

Measured using the Brier score... the same method used to evaluate weather forecasters and intelligence analysts. A well-calibrated mind is a more effective mind.

The Dunning-Kruger Effect

First documented by Kruger & Dunning (1999) at Cornell. People with limited knowledge in a domain systematically overestimate their competence. Awareness is the first step out.

The Research

Journal of Personality and Social Psychology
Unskilled and Unaware of It
Kruger & Dunning · 1999
The original paper establishing that low performers systematically overestimate their own competence.
Psychonomic Bulletin & Review
Dunning-Kruger Effects in Reasoning
Pennycook, Ross, Koehler & Fugelsang · 2017
Replication and extension of the DK effect specifically in reasoning and logic tasks.
Judgment Under Uncertainty — Cambridge University Press
Calibration of Probabilities: The State of the Art to 1980
Lichtenstein, Fischhoff & Phillips · 1982
Foundational review showing that subjective confidence systematically misaligns with actual accuracy rates.
Psychological Review
The Trouble with Overconfidence
Moore & Healy · 2008
Distinguishes three forms of overconfidence... overprecision is the one DK Derby's confidence score directly measures.
Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B
The Neural Basis of Metacognitive Ability
Fleming & Dolan · 2012
Confidence calibration reflects a measurable metacognitive skill rooted in identifiable prefrontal brain systems.
Journal of Experimental Psychology: General
Monitoring One's Own Knowledge During Study
Koriat · 1997
People rely on fluency and familiarity as proxies for knowledge... the mechanism behind confidently wrong answers.
Monthly Weather Review
Verification of Forecasts Expressed in Terms of Probability
Brier · 1950
The original paper defining the Brier score... the proper scoring rule DK Derby uses to measure calibration quality.
Perception & Psychophysics
The Calibration and Resolution of Confidence in Perceptual Judgments
Baranski & Petrusic · 1994
Trial-by-trial feedback measurably improves alignment between confidence and accuracy... calibration is a trainable skill.
